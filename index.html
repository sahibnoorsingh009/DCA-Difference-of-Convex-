How DCA Works

  1. Linearize: Replace h(x) = |x| with its linear approximation at current point
  2. Solve: Minimize the convex approximation g(x) - linear(x)
  3. Update: Move to the minimum of the convex problem
  4. Repeat: Continue until convergence

The genius of DCA is transforming a non-convex problem into a sequence of convex problems. Each iteration gets us closer to the global minimum at x = 0!
